After a browser is done transforming the markup's text to the DOM tree, AngularJS kicks in and traverses the parsed DOM structure.
Each time it encounters a directive, AngularJS executes its logic to turn directives into dynamic parts of the screen.

It is almost similar to creating a new domain-specific language (DSL) on top of HTML and instructing a browser on how to make sense of the new instructions.
You can often hear that AngularJS "teaches browsers new tricks".
